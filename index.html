FAQs

What is Belto Doc?

Belto Doc is an AI-powered writing workspace where students learn with structure — not shortcuts. Professors define how AI behaves, ensuring submissions follow academic rules while promoting real learning.

How is Belto different from ChatGPT or other AI tools?

Belto doesn’t just generate content — it enforces learning policies. Students can’t override behavior, use banned sources, or bypass your rules. It’s AI built for education, not open-ended prompting.

Can I control how students use the AI?

Yes. You can set prompt limits, control generation length, disable citations, restrict certain formats, and even define the tone of responses. Think of it as classroom-specific AI governance — built into your LMS.

Does Belto integrate with our LMS?

Yes. Belto integrates seamlessly with major LMS platforms like Canvas, Blackboard, and Moodle — so you can manage AI compliance without changing your workflow.

What kind of AI powers Belto?

Each class gets its own AI assistant, trained exclusively on your uploaded lecture materials. Students can ask questions, reflect on concepts, and get writing guidance — all within your academic guardrails.

Can Belto support group projects?

Absolutely. Professors can enable collaborative sessions where students work together under shared AI and submission rules — just like in real classrooms, but smarter.

Is Belto secure and FERPA-compliant?

Yes. Belto is built with security-first architecture and adheres to FERPA compliance standards. Student data is never shared, sold, or used to train external models.
